
---

🧠 Core Strategy (Before UI)

Most people mess this up.

You have 2 audiences, not one:

End users → emotional + visual

Distributors → logical + profit-driven


👉 Your site must look like it’s for consumers
👉 But convert like it’s for distributors

That tension is the whole game.


---

🧱 1. Homepage Wireframe (Conversion-first)

🔷 Section 1: Hero (Above the fold)

Goal: Instantly look like a legit FMCG brand (not a local supplier)

Content:

Headline:

> “Powerful Cleaning. Trusted Everyday.”



Subtext:

> “Dorron by Aquaglow — effective, affordable, and made for modern homes.”



CTAs:

“Explore Products”

“Become a Distributor”



👉 Two CTAs = you’re acknowledging both audiences early


---

🔷 Section 2: Product Categories (Visual grid)

Layout: Grid of 4–6 categories:

Dishwash Liquid

Toilet Cleaner

Floor Cleaner

Bathroom Cleaner

Detergent

Fabric Conditioner


Each card:

Product image

One-liner benefit
(e.g. “Tough on grease, gentle on hands”)


👉 Keep it skimmable. No essays.


---

🔷 Section 3: Why Dorron? (Trust builder)

3–4 pillars:

Effective cleaning performance

Value for money

Safe for daily use

Consistent quality


👉 Don’t claim “premium luxury.”
👉 You’re competing on reliability + price-performance


---

🔷 Section 4: Distributor Pitch (THIS is your money section)

This is where most small brands fail.

Headline:

> “Partner With a Growing Cleaning Brand”



Points:

High demand FMCG category

Attractive margins

Consistent supply

Expanding product range


CTA: 👉 “Apply for Distribution”


---

🔷 Section 5: About Snapshot

Short, no drama:

> “Aquaglow is a manufacturer of high-quality cleaning solutions. With Dorron, we aim to deliver reliable, effective products for everyday use across households and businesses.”



👉 No “vision mission revolutionizing industry” nonsense.


---

🔷 Section 6: Contact / Inquiry CTA

Simple:

Phone

Email

WhatsApp (important for India)

Form

✍️ 3. Copy Direction (No corporate cringe)

Tone:

Simple

Clear

Functional

Slightly aspirational


Avoid:

❌ “Revolutionary cleaning solutions”
❌ “World-class innovation”
❌ “Cutting-edge technology”

Use:

✔ “Removes tough stains easily”
✔ “Designed for everyday use”
✔ “Reliable performance”


---

🎨 4. Visual Direction

Style:

Bright

Clean

White backgrounds

Slight color accents based on product


Look inspiration:

Not Unilever level premium

Not local wholesale catalog either


👉 Aim for: “Mid-tier modern FMCG brand”


---

Image Strategy:

You already did something smart:

AI lifestyle visuals → keep

Packaging → highlight


Use both:

Lifestyle = emotion

Product = trust



---

⚠️ Brutal Reality Check

Let me push back a bit.

If this site becomes:

Too fancy → distributors won’t care

Too basic → no brand trust


You need: 👉 “Looks good enough to trust” 👉 “Simple enough to act fast”


---

🧩 Missing Piece (Important)

You didn’t mention:

👉 Pricing positioning 👉 Target market level (low / mid / premium)

This affects EVERYTHING:

copy

visuals

messaging


Right now I assumed mid-market value brand.

If that’s wrong, this entire strategy shifts.


---

🚀 What Should Happen Next

Don’t jump to design yet.

Next step should be: 👉 Lock positioning in one line:

Example:

“Affordable daily cleaning solutions”

“Premium hygiene for modern homes”

“Value-first bulk cleaning products”
